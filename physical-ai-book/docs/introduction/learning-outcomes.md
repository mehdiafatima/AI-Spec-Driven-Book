# Course Goals & Learning Outcomes

This section outlines the primary goals of the "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics" course and the specific learning outcomes students are expected to achieve upon its completion.

## Course Goals

The overarching goals of this course are to:

1.  **Provide a Foundational Understanding**: Equip students with a solid theoretical and practical understanding of Physical AI, embodied intelligence, and humanoid robotics.
2.  **Develop Practical Skills**: Foster hands-on experience in designing, implementing, and controlling physical AI systems using industry-standard tools and platforms (e.g., ROS 2, Gazebo, NVIDIA Isaac Sim).
3.  **Cultivate Critical Thinking**: Encourage students to critically analyze the challenges and opportunities in physical AI, including ethical considerations, safety, and real-world deployment.
4.  **Promote Innovation**: Inspire students to explore novel solutions and contribute to the advancements in humanoid robotics and intelligent autonomous systems.
5.  **Prepare for Future Careers**: Prepare students for careers in robotics engineering, AI research, automation, and related fields.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

### Knowledge and Understanding:

-   **LO1.1**: Describe the core concepts of Physical AI and differentiate it from traditional AI.
-   **LO1.2**: Explain the theory of embodied intelligence and its implications for robot design and control.
-   **LO1.3**: Identify the key components of a humanoid robot system, including sensors, actuators, and control architectures.
-   **LO1.4**: Understand the principles of ROS 2 for robot communication, software modularity, and package management.
-   **LO1.5**: Grasp the concepts of digital twins and their application in simulating and validating physical AI systems.
-   **LO1.6**: Comprehend the functionalities and workflows of advanced simulation platforms like NVIDIA Isaac Sim.
-   **LO1.7**: Discuss the current state and future trends in humanoid robotics, including research on VLA (Vision-Language-Action) models.

### Practical and Intellectual Skills:

-   **LO2.1**: Configure and set up a development environment for ROS 2 and other robotics software.
-   **LO2.2**: Implement basic ROS 2 nodes, topics, services, and actions for robot control.
-   **LO2.3**: Model robotic systems using URDF and simulate their behavior in Gazebo.
-   **LO2.4**: Develop and integrate simulation workflows using NVIDIA Isaac Sim for complex robotic tasks.
-   **LO2.5**: Analyze sensor data and implement basic perception algorithms for environmental understanding.
-   **LO2.6**: Design and execute experiments to evaluate the performance of physical AI systems.

### Transferable Skills:

-   **LO3.1**: Work effectively in teams on robotics projects.
-   **LO3.2**: Communicate technical concepts clearly through written reports and presentations.
-   **LO3.3**: Solve complex engineering problems systematically and creatively.
-   **LO3.4**: Adapt to new technologies and programming paradigms in the rapidly evolving field of AI and robotics.
-   **LO3.5**: Critically assess ethical and societal implications of deploying autonomous physical AI systems.
